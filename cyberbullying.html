Here's a little explanation of what cyber-bullying is. Bullying may seem easy to just define at first, but delving into the topic reveals that there are many other parts to it. Bullying does not have to involve teenagers, or even happen in schools, as can cyber-bullying. The main premise of bullying is when one person uses an imbalance of perceived power to cause to a person's physical or mental health. As you can see, it's a pretty broad definition, but that is because there are so many ways that a person can be bullies. Cyber-bullying is when bullying takes place online. It can happen through social media, email, messaging, and any platform that a person can publish a part of their identity or their work and communicate with others.
